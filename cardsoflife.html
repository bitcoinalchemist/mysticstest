Every card in a spread sits beneath a planetary influence. These are the seven planets as Olney H. Richmond gave them in The Mystic Test Book, each with its light and its shadow, and the Crown that presides over them all.

You have carried a calendar in your pocket all your life and called it a game.

Look again at the deck. The measures of the year were counted into it long before anyone thought to keep score, and the fit is exact:

  • 52 cards=52 weeks of the year
  • 5 + 2=7 days of the week
  • 4 suits=4 seasons & 4 classical elements
  • 12 court cards=12 months & 12 zodiac signs
  • 13 ranks per suit=13 lunar months per year
  • 91 pips per suit=91 days per season
  • 91 × 4=364 days
  • 52 × 7=364 days
  • The Joker=the 5 elements at a quarter day each, the 1¼ left over
  • 364 + 1¼=365¼ days, the solar year
  • 2 colours=day & night, yin & yang

Exact to the quarter day. Whoever shaped the deck was watching the sun.

Echoes of the Count

Once you have seen the year in the deck, the same numbers begin to sound elsewhere. Sit at a piano and count: fifty-two white keys, thirty-six black, eighty-eight in all. Raise your eyes at night and the sky answers in kind.

  • 52 white piano keys=the 52 cards, and the 52 northern constellations
  • 36 black piano keys=the 36 decans of the zodiac, and the 36 southern constellations
  • 88 keys in all=the 88 constellations that cover the whole sky
  • 88 quadrations=lie between the Spirit and the Life spread
  • "Red" & "Black"=each counts to 11 in Chaldean numerology
  • "Red", "Black" & "White"=together count to 53

Once is chance. Counted this many ways, it is architecture. You were born on one day of this measured year, and that day is a card. Everything on this page unfolds from it.

Your Birth Card

Your birthday counts you into the deck through the Solar Value: sv = 55 − (2 × month + day). The count lands on one of the 52 cards, and that card is yours. Its days are your days, its planets keep your seasons, its company is the company you keep. One date alone stands outside the count: December 31 belongs to the Joker, who holds no seat and answers to no planet. The Finder at the top of the page will look you up.

Spreads and Quadrations

Laid out in full, the deck becomes a chart: seven planetary rows of seven cards beneath a crown of three masters. A fixed reshuffle called a Quadration reseats every card, and ninety Quadrations carry the deck home to where it began. So every age from 0 to 89 keeps a spread of its own. Step the age selector and watch your influences cross the board over a lifetime.

The Life Script

Each column of the spread belongs to a planet. Read across the seven, Mercury through Neptune, and the cards you pass are your Life Script: your quickness, your loves, your strength, your fortune, your trials, your labour and your far horizons. The Planets tool holds the full portrait of each.

Spirit and Life

The deck keeps two master seatings. The Spirit spread is its perfect order, the deck as it was first conceived. The Life spread is the order it settles into here, in the world. Crossing between them, every card takes another's seat and yields its own, and three hold fast through the whole exchange: the Jack of Hearts, the Eight of Clubs and the King of Spades. Flip the spread above between Annual, Spirit and Life and watch the trade.

Relationships

Bring a second birthday to the Finder and the two Solar Values meet in a third card: the card of the relationship itself, with its own character, its own gifts and its own lessons to teach.
